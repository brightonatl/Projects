Part 1: Pitch & Plan

Minor Projects (15 Marks Total)

Minor projects are exciting brainstorming tasks! You only need to pitch a super cool science or robotics idea. 🎉 No physical hardware building is needed yet! It is strictly a SOFTCOPY (PPT/PDF/DOCX) submission. You will design, plan, and detail your concept on slides.

Step-by-Step Submission Guide:

  • 1
    Build your idea file using PowerPoint (.PPT), PDF, or Word (.DOCX) formats.
  • 2
    Fill up the easy submission Google Form linked directly in the header menu.
  • 3
    Upload your file directly inside the form OR hand over a digital copy to your respected Robotics Teacher.

🌟 The Golden Rule of Transition 🌟

To unlock Major Project status, you must keep the exact same idea you submitted in your Minor softcopy slides. Your primary goal is to turn those sketches into a real, working physical machine!

Part 2: Real-World Prototyping

Major Projects (20 Marks Total)

This is where the real fun begins! You will take the exact list of sensors and costing parameters outlined in your Minor proposal to construct your physical system. Solder modules, configure microcontroller code loops, calibrate variables, and wrap up your components neatly.

🎮 Teammates: Work with the exact same partners listed in your initial Minor softcopy submission.
📦 Lab Stock Desk: Reference your costing sheet to check out active microcontrollers and sensors from coordinates.
🔥 Final Deliverable: Present a completely functioning, cleanly integrated physical prototype with live diagnostics.
